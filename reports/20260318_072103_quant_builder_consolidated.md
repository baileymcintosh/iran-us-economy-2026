### A. Kharg Island Capacity Analysis (A_c264475b)

The **March 13 US strike on Kharg Island was surgical — military targets only, oil infrastructure deliberately spared.** Key facts from open sources:
- Kharg handles **90% of Iran's crude exports**, loading capacity up to **7M bpd** (actual exports ~1.5M bpd)
- The strike destroyed **90 military targets** on the island but left export terminals intact
- Iran maintains current exports of **1.1–1.5M bpd through Kharg**
- However, Iran has effectively **closed the Strait of Hormuz** — through which 20% of global oil trade flows — making Kharg's survival moot for near-term export volumes
- The **Goreh-Jask pipeline** (capacity ~1.5M bpd) bypasses both Kharg and Hormuz but represents a strategic hedge, not a live substitute
- **Net direct Kharg export loss: ~0 bpd** (infrastructure intact). **Indirect Hormuz disruption: IEA estimates 8M bpd global supply shock**
